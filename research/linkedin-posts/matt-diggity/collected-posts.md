## Post 2: AI Visibility and SEO Fundamentals

Original URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/mattdiggityseo_you-dont-need-to-chase-every-new-aiseo-activity-7368464470781685760-_oYM  
Date published: Unknown

### Why this source matters

This post is relevant because it connects AI visibility with core SEO fundamentals instead of treating AI search as a completely separate channel.

### Raw content

You don't need to chase every new AISEO “hack”. 

The 80/20 of AI search comes from doubling down on SEO fundamentals.

Here's what actually works for AI visibility in 2025:

1. Get mentioned across the web

AI picks up brand mentions, with or without backlinks. Focus on:

- Industry "best of" lists
- Customer reviews and case studies
- PR coverage and media mentions

2. Create content AI loves to cite

Ahrefs’ analysis shows these content types dominate AI citations:

- "Best" content: 7.06% of AI traffic (comprehensive buying guides work well)
- How-to guides: 6.35% of AI traffic (step-by-step tutorials with clear outcomes)
- "Top" lists: 5.5% of AI traffic (data-backed rankings and comparisons)
- Product comparisons: 4.88% of AI traffic ("vs" content addressing specific use cases)

3. Structure for AI comprehension

Make it dead simple for AI to parse and cite your content:

- Use clear H1, H2, H3 headings that mirror how people ask questions
- Add bullet points and short paragraphs (avoid text walls)
- Include specific facts and figures AI can cite directly
- Answer common questions explicitly with factual statements
- Add schema markup for FAQs and product information

4. Dominate YouTube and Reddit

These are the 2nd and 3rd most cited domains in AI responses… yet most people ignore them.

YouTube optimization:

- Create tutorials and product demos with clear timestamps
- Use keyword-rich titles and descriptions
- Add detailed video descriptions AI can parse
- Focus on "how to" and comparison content

Reddit strategy:

- Find high-traffic Reddit threads already ranking on Google
- Add thoughtful, helpful answers (not promotional content)
- Focus on product discussion subreddits in your niche

5. Track your AI mention gaps

Use tools like Ahrefs' Brand Radar to see where competitors get AI citations but you don't. 

Then create better content targeting those same topics:

- Create deeper, more authoritative content 
- Add original data, case studies, or expert quotes 
- Update existing content with fresh statistics and examples 

The reality?

Good content, clear structure, and solid authority signals work for both traditional SEO and AI search.

You're not starting over. You're just expanding your definition of success beyond clicks to include AI citations.

### Research notes

- Main idea: AI visibility still depends on strong SEO basics.
- Useful for future playbook: Good reminder that AI content workflows should not ignore search intent, authority, structure, and quality.
- Tactical insight: Improve existing SEO foundations before chasing every new AI search tactic.
- Risk / limitation: Marketers may over-focus on AI hacks and neglect content quality or site authority.
- Possible application for B2B SaaS: Build strong product/category pages first, then layer AI visibility tactics on top.
