### Why this source matters

This post is relevant because it shows the tool stack behind modern SEO and AI search workflows.

### Raw content

AI and SEO tools I use every day:

1. Rankability - Optimize for AI search + tracking AI search performance (public release soon)

2. Replit - Build HTML-driven websites, prototypes for software, and free tools. It's scary good when you know how to use it.

3. ChatGPT / Gemini - Data analysis, editing (anything), coding, brainstorming, improve clarity of copy, etc

4. Canva - YouTube thumbs, quick graphics, decks

5. Microsoft Clarity - UX analysis

6. Google Search Console - Daily check for new, untapped topic opportunities. The new Keyword Finder 2.0 in Rankability will be fully integrated with GSC

7. Whitespark - The most beautiful local grid tracking on the market

### Research notes

- Main idea: AI SEO is a system of tools and workflows, not a single prompt.
- Useful for future playbook: Can support a section on building a practical AI SEO operating system.
- Tactical insight: Different tools can support analysis, content optimization, prototyping, editing, and measurement.
- Risk / limitation: Too many tools can create scattered workflows without a clear process.
- Possible application for B2B SaaS: Build a simple content production stack for research, outline, draft, optimize, publish, and measure.
