## Post 1: Choosing AI Models for Automation

Original URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/gael-breton_think-openai-is-the-king-for-automation-activity-7326589391483621376-p8Cf  
Date published: Unknown  
Topic tags: AI automation, workflow design, API cost, content operations  

### Why this source matters

This post is relevant because AI-powered content production depends on workflow design, model choice, and automation cost, not only prompts.

### Raw content

Think OpenAI is the king for automation? ❌ WRONG.

It might be the WORST model you can choose for your API calls. 

Here's the truth...

I get asked constantly: "Gael, which AI models should I be using for my projects? Which are the best? The most cost-effective?"

After implementing AI in numerous real businesses, I've got some strong opinions. If you want to get more out of AI and save a BUNCH of money, listen up.

👉 The Biggest Mistake? 

Using GPT-4o (from ChatGPT) for Automations.

Seriously. I know it's familiar, but for API use and automation, it's:

- Expensive ($2.5 in/$10 out per million tokens)
- Got a relatively small context window (128k tokens)
- Not the smartest on the block (scores low on intelligence benchmarks for its price)

If you take one thing away: Do NOT use GPT-4o for your backend automations. It's likely the worst value for that purpose.

So, what SHOULD you use?

🚀 S-TIER for Simple, Scalable Tasks: Gemini 2.0 Flash (not 2.5)

Why? 

- It's INSANELY cheap (think $0.10/$0.40 per million tokens vs GPT-4o's $5/$20).
- Perfect for high-volume, simple tasks: summaries, extracting info, filtering leads, etc.
- Decent intelligence for the price, image generation, search grounding. 

My bills for this are tiny.

🚀 S-TIER for Top-Quality Answers at Scale: Gemini 2.5 Pro

Why? 

If you need the smartest responses without completely breaking the bank, this is my go-to.

Performance is neck-and-neck with models like OpenAI's O3, but at a fraction of the cost (e.g., $1.25 in/$10 out vs O3's $10 in/$40 out).

This is the model I turn to when I need the best quality outputs for complex tasks.

Other Quick Thoughts:

Claude (Sonnet models): Good writing quality, but often too pricey for API, especially when reasoning is involved. Used to be a leader, but value has slipped for automations.

OpenAI's other API models (O4 Mini, GPT-4.1): Have their places. O4 Mini can be a decent mini-reasoner (though watch output costs), and GPT-4.1 is good for AI agents/tool calling. But often, Google's models offer better overall value.

The Bottom Line:

What's popular in a chatbot isn't always what's best or most economical for building automations. 

For API access and getting real work done efficiently, Google's models are frequently giving the best bang for your buck right now.

What are your go-to AI models for automation, and did any of my picks surprise you?

### Research notes

- Main idea: The best AI model for automation is not always the most famous model.
- Useful for future playbook: Adds a practical operations angle to AI content production.
- Tactical insight: Choose AI models based on cost, speed, accuracy, and task type.
- Risk / limitation: Using the wrong model at scale can make workflows expensive or unreliable.
- Possible application for B2B SaaS: Use cheaper/faster models for repetitive research or formatting tasks, and stronger models for strategic editing.

## Post 2: Authority Hacker Business Model Shift

Original URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/gael-breton_after-10-years-of-teaching-seo-were-closing-activity-7274768322427047936-SdfM  
Date published: Unknown  
Topic tags: content sites, affiliate SEO, business model, AI disruption  

### Why this source matters

This post is relevant because it reflects how SEO education and content-site businesses are adapting as AI changes search and marketing workflows.

### Raw content

After 10 years of teaching SEO, we're closing our flagship courses.

And no, SEO isn't dead. But the content site model is.

When Mark Webster and I started Authority Hacker in 2014, building content sites was the perfect business model for new entrepreneurs.

✅ Low barrier to entry. 
✅ Predictable growth. 
✅ Solid ROI.

But in 2024 and going forward? 

That model is broken for most people.

I've been to countless SEO events recently where everyone keeps a straight face and pretends everything is fine.

I won't do that (and we've called Google out all year in our news videos).

The Google updates, AI content tsunami, and dramatic SERP changes have fundamentally altered the game.

And unlike previous updates where we could adapt and overcome, this time is different.

So we're doing something that feels scary but right:

We're closing TASS and AH Pro, our main courses that generated 8 figures in revenue.

Why?

Because I refuse to sell courses teaching strategies that no longer work reliably for most people.

What's next for Authority Hacker?

Honestly? 

We don't know yet.

But we've always been about finding what actually works in digital marketing and sharing it with brutal honesty.

That won't change.

The podcast will continue. 

The free content will flow (but expect some changes in direction next year). 

And when we find the next big opportunity, you'll be the first to know.

To everyone who's been part of this journey since 2014:

Thank you. 

The skills you've learned about website building, content creation, and digital marketing are still valuable.

They just need to be applied differently now.

Sometimes the best way forward is to admit when something's not working anymore and have the courage to try something new.

❤️ to our incredible team that has helped us throughout the journey.

Here's to the next chapter. 🚀

### Research notes

- Main idea: AI is changing not only content production but also SEO education and content business models.
- Useful for future playbook: Shows why AI-powered SEO should be connected to business outcomes, not just publishing volume.
- Tactical insight: Content strategies need to adapt when search behavior, tools, and traffic sources change.
- Risk / limitation: Old SEO playbooks can become less effective if they rely only on traditional affiliate/content-site assumptions.
- Possible application for B2B SaaS: Build content systems that support lead generation, authority, and distribution instead of relying only on organic clicks.
