## Post 2: How Google’s AI Mode Works and Why SEO Is Not Enough

Original URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/michaelkingphilly_how-ai-mode-works-and-how-seo-can-prepare-activity-7333250164985434112-KJyj  
Date published: Unknown  
Topic tags: AI search, SEO strategy, AI Mode, content strategy  

### Why this source matters

This post is relevant because it challenges the idea that traditional SEO alone is enough in an AI search environment.

### Raw content

Ok, here it is. My deep dive into the various patents that underpin how Google's AI Mode works.

https://lnkd.in/dC3Pvpfy 

The main thought I came away after this analysis is that search results are no longer deterministic in this environment. They are highly probabilistic due to the LLM-driven reasoning chains. This inherently means that the things we're doing don't always work anymore because our content is at the mercy of what an LLM "decides" at numerous stages that we can't see. 

For me, that definitively puts the "it's just SEO" argument to bed.

It's going to require engineering, specifically Relevance Engineering, for us to navigate this and figure out how to ensure we make it through the reasoning cascade and reliably drive results.

Oh, and I think Query Fan Out is the place to start, so I included a tool called Qforia. It's my first attempt at replicating 6 out of the 7 synthetic query styles that Google generates (yep, I found the Query Fan-out patent too).

I'm looking forward to continued discussion. Well, the non "it's just SEO" parts of it, at least. I'm eager for us to collectively land on what it's going to take for us to thrive in this new environment. Naturally, I provided my initial thoughts on how to structure content based on what we've seen be effective and how the systems work, but we'll need to test things out as a community to build the new common practices.

### Research notes

- Main idea: AI search expands what SEO teams need to optimize for.
- Useful for future playbook: Can support a section on how AI search changes SEO measurement and content strategy.
- Tactical insight: SEO teams need to think about retrieval, citations, source authority, and entity clarity.
- Risk / limitation: It may be hard to directly attribute AI search visibility to one content action.
- Possible application for B2B SaaS: Combine traditional SEO with brand/entity building and third-party validation.
