## Post 1: How and When to Use AI for Content Strategy

Original URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/andycrestodina_how-and-when-to-use-ai-for-content-strategy-activity-7470795727464759297-8gAB  
Date published: Unknown  
Topic tags: AI content strategy, B2B content, SEO, analytics  

### Why this source matters

This post is relevant because it positions AI as a support tool for strategy, research, and analysis instead of a full replacement for human content judgment.

### Raw content

Trick question: "Do you use AI for content marketing?"
It's tricky because creating content isn't just one thing. It's many things.

This updated guide goes into five of the most effective ways to use AI for content strategy... all of them go beyond writing.

Today, we're sharing five methods (and AI prompts) for more performant, compelling, memorable content.

1️⃣ AI for auditing your blog’s calls to action (maximize list growth)
2️⃣ AI for topic research (focus on the three best formats for content)
3️⃣ AI for initial edits (alignment with content best practices)
4️⃣ AI for finding partners and places to publish (PR and outreach ideas)
5️⃣ AI for performance analysis (find topic/channel fit)

My favorite: the second method. The responses are hilarious. In minutes, you'll have a list of irresistible topics for your audience, impossible for them to scroll past.

My second favorite: the first method. Before you hit "publish" on another article, run the first method. If you can improve the call-to-action to subscribe, all of your content will be more effective forever after.

Scan through, pick your own favorites, add the promps to your swipe file ...and add them to your workflows. 

There's more to AI than "Write me an article about X." 

In fact, that's probably the worst way to use AI. Anything generated by the simple prompt should never be published. If AI can make it without the help of a human ...it should not be written at all.

We've written and rewritten this guide over three years. This time, it got a major overhaul: better prompts with better outputs. 

Definitely worth a quick scan...

Enjoy!

And big thanks to my friend and collaborator Liza Adams. Liza is a leader in this stuff. Follow her if you aren't yet!

### Research notes

- Main idea: AI is most useful when it supports content strategy and analysis.
- Useful for future playbook: Helps build a balanced AI content workflow that does not rely on blind automation.
- Tactical insight: Use AI for research, audits, ideation, and analysis before using it to draft content.
- Risk / limitation: Fully outsourcing content creation to AI can weaken originality and trust.
- Possible application for B2B SaaS: Use AI to analyze customer questions, competitors, and content gaps before writing.
