# Methodology

## Topic

AI-powered SEO content production for B2B SaaS.

## Goal

The goal of this project is to understand how strong SEO, content, and growth practitioners are using AI across the SEO content production workflow.

This research is not meant to be a final playbook yet. It is the research foundation for a future workflow or playbook that a B2B SaaS team could use to plan, create, optimize, distribute, and measure AI-assisted SEO content.

The research was designed to answer questions such as:

- How should a B2B SaaS company choose SEO content topics?
- Where can AI help in the content workflow?
- Where is human editing and judgment still important?
- How do marketers avoid low-quality AI-generated content?
- How should content be optimized for traditional SEO and AI search?
- How should content be distributed after publishing?
- How should results be measured?

---



## Why This Topic Matters

B2B SaaS companies often rely on content to educate buyers, build trust, and capture organic demand. But strong content production takes time, especially when the topic requires research, examples, editing, SEO optimization, and distribution.

AI can speed up parts of this process, but it should not replace strategy or judgment. Poor AI content can become generic, inaccurate, or disconnected from the buyer’s real problems.

That is why this project focuses on AI as part of a content workflow, not just as a writing shortcut.

## Expert Selection Criteria

I selected experts using the following criteria:

1. **Practitioner, not only commentator**
  I prioritized people who actively work in SEO, content marketing, AI search, content distribution, or content operations.
2. **Direct relevance to AI-powered SEO content production**
  I looked for experts whose content connects to AI SEO, AI search, AEO/GEO, content workflows, content quality, distribution, or SEO systems.
3. **Public content available**
  I selected people with public LinkedIn posts, YouTube videos, webinars, blogs, podcasts, or resource pages that could be collected and organized.
4. **Useful for B2B SaaS or content-led growth**
  I prioritized sources that could help a SaaS company build a practical content workflow, not just generic AI tips.
5. **Tactical insight over hype**
  I avoided choosing people only because they talk about AI. I looked for people who explain workflows, tradeoffs, experiments, frameworks, or real marketing applications.

## Source Types Collected

I organized the research into three main source types.

### LinkedIn Posts

LinkedIn posts were collected manually because LinkedIn access is limited and the quality of each post needed manual review.

For each expert, I collected relevant posts related to topics such as:

- AI SEO
- AI search
- AEO / GEO
- SEO content production
- Content quality
- Content distribution
- B2B content strategy
- Workflow design

Each author’s posts are stored inside:

```text
research/linkedin-posts/
```



### YouTube Transcripts

YouTube transcripts were collected where available and organized by video.

The transcripts are useful because they provide long-form explanations, examples, interviews, tutorials, and expert commentary.

Each video transcript is stored inside:

```text
research/youtube-transcripts/
```

Some transcript formatting was cleaned with local Python scripts so timestamps were easier to read in Markdown.

### Other Materials

I also collected supporting materials such as:

Articles
Blog posts
Checklists
Webinar pages
Podcast pages
Official resource pages

These materials are stored inside:

```text
research/other/
```

I did not copy full articles into the repo. For supporting materials, I saved the source link, a short summary, and quick research notes.

### Collection Approach

I used a mix of manual research and lightweight automation.

Expert selection was done manually.
LinkedIn posts were collected manually.
YouTube transcripts were collected using transcript tools or manual transcript collection when needed.
Python scripts were used to help format and normalize transcripts.
Supporting materials were collected from official websites, blogs, webinars, podcast pages, and public resources.

I prioritized fewer high-signal sources over collecting a large volume of generic content.

What I Looked For

While reviewing sources, I looked for ideas related to:

Topic selection
Search intent
Content briefs
AI-assisted research
AI-assisted drafting
Editing and fact-checking
Content optimization
AEO / GEO
AI search visibility
Content distribution
Repurposing
Measurement
B2B SaaS application
Quality Control

To keep the research useful, I tried to avoid:

Generic AI content advice
Pure hype around AI tools
Sources with no clear SEO or content marketing experience
Content that only talks about producing more articles faster
Sources that were not relevant to B2B SaaS, SEO, AI search, or content-led growth

I focused on sources that could support a future practical playbook.

## Limitations

This project was completed within a limited time window, so I focused on quality instead of volume.

Some experts have more public content than others. Some YouTube videos may not have clean transcripts available. LinkedIn posts were collected manually, so this is not a complete dataset of every post from each expert.

The research should be treated as a curated source collection, not a full academic dataset.
