# Methodology

## Topic

AI-powered SEO content production for B2B SaaS.

## Goal

The goal of this project is to understand how strong SEO and content marketers are using AI in the content production process.

I wanted to collect useful sources that could help answer questions like:

- How should a B2B SaaS company choose topics?
- Where can AI help in the SEO content workflow?
- Where is human editing still important?
- How do marketers avoid low-quality AI content?
- How should content be optimized, distributed, and measured?

## Why This Topic Matters

B2B SaaS companies often use content to educate buyers and bring in organic traffic. But content production can take a lot of time, especially when the topic needs research, examples, editing, and SEO optimization.

AI can help speed up some parts of the process, but it should not replace strategy or judgment. Bad AI content can be generic, inaccurate, or not useful. Good AI-assisted content still needs a clear audience, good sources, editing, and a reason to exist.

That is why I focused on AI as part of a content workflow, not just as a writing shortcut.

## How I Chose Experts

I looked for people who have practical experience in at least one of these areas:

- SEO strategy
- B2B SaaS content
- AI search
- Content operations
- Content distribution
- Search quality and Google updates

I avoided choosing people only because they talk about AI. I tried to pick people who have real experience and useful public content.

## How I Collected Sources

### LinkedIn

LinkedIn posts were collected manually from public profiles. For each post, I saved the link, date if available, summary, and why the post is relevant.

### YouTube

For YouTube videos, I collected transcripts when available. I also added short notes and takeaways so the transcript is easier to use later.

### Other Sources

I also included useful blogs, newsletters, podcasts, and public resources when they added something practical to the research.

## What I Looked For

While reviewing sources, I looked for ideas related to:

- Topic selection
- Search intent
- Content briefs
- AI-assisted drafting
- Editing and fact-checking
- SEO optimization
- Distribution
- Measuring results
- AI search visibility

## Limitations

This project is limited by time, so I focused on quality instead of volume.

Some experts have more public content than others. Some YouTube videos may not have transcripts available. LinkedIn posts were collected manually, so this is not a complete dataset.

## Next Step

The next step would be to turn the research into a simple playbook or workflow that a B2B SaaS team could actually use.
